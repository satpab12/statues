Venus de Milo

The Venus de Milo is an ancient Greek marble sculpture that has captivated art enthusiasts and historians for centuries. Created around 100 BC, the statue depicts the goddess Aphrodite (Venus in Roman mythology). Standing at 6 feet 8 inches tall, it is renowned for its exquisite beauty and elegant composition. One of the most striking features of the Venus de Milo is the missing arms, which has fueled speculation and intrigue about its original form and purpose. Today, the statue is displayed at the Louvre Museum in Paris, where it continues to draw countless visitors who marvel at its timeless grace and enigmatic allure.
